Question 10 — The memorable ID scheme: which entities, and what format?

You want random-but-memorable IDs (random string + memorable noun). Let me pin the format and scope so it's consistent.

My recommendation: a prefixed, human-readable scheme across all entities, so an ID tells you both what it is and is easy to say out loud in a demo: - Endpoints: ep_brave-otter-7f3a - Events: evt_calm-river-2b91 - Deliveries: dlv_swift-hawk-9c20 - Attempts: plain integer/autoincrement (internal, never spoken) — or att_... if you want consistency.

Format: <prefix>_<adjective>-<noun>-<4 hex>. The adjective-noun gives memorability; the short hex suffix guarantees uniqueness without needing a huge wordlist or collision checks. This is the Heroku/Docker-style naming people already find friendly.

The one caution: the delivered envelope's id (Q8) is the Event id and it's a consumer-facing dedup key — memorable is fine, but it must stay globally unique and stable across retries (the hex suffix handles that).

Two to confirm: (1) prefixed memorable IDs for endpoints/events/deliveries (attempts stay integer), and (2) the <prefix>_<adj>-<noun>-<hex> format — good, or do you want memorable names only for Endpoints (since those are the ones humans pick from a list) and opaque IDs elsewhere?
