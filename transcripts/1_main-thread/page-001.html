Question 1 — The word "webhook" is already overloaded in your brain dump.

In your brainstorming.md you use "webhook" to mean the registered URL (webhooks: id, url, ...), but then also talk about "delivering the event to the webhook url" and "invoke each registered webhook." That's at least three different concepts riding on one word:

  1. The registered destination (the URL + its config — secret, etc.)
  2. The act of delivering one event to one destination (the row in event_logs)
  3. The HTTP request itself (one attempt, which may be retried)

If you don't separate these now, your schema, your API routes, and your dashboard tabs will all inherit the ambiguity. The industry tends to split them as Endpoint/Subscription (the destination), Delivery (the event→destination pairing, the unit you track and retry), and Attempt (a single HTTP try).

My recommendation: adopt three canonical terms —

  • Subscription — a registered destination URL (+ secret, + which events it wants). Replaces your webhooks table.
  • Delivery — one event headed to one subscription; has a status and a retry count. Replaces event_logs.
  • Attempt — one HTTP request made for a delivery; has a response/status code. (You may or may not store these separately — that's a later question.)

Do you want to adopt that vocabulary, or do you have a reason to keep "webhook" as the primary noun? And does "Subscription" feel right to you, or would you prefer "Endpoint"?

# Context

A glossary of the domain language for the webhook delivery service. Implementation
details do not belong here — only the meaning of terms.

## Glossary

### Endpoint
A registered destination for events: a URL plus its configuration (e.g. secret,
which events it wants). Persisted; created when a caller registers a destination.
Chosen over "Subscription" and over the overloaded term "webhook".

### Event
Something that happened, ingested into the system. Gets validated and persisted,
then fanned out to matching Endpoints.

### Delivery
One Event headed to one Endpoint. The unit we track and retry. Has a status
(e.g. pending → delivered/failed) and a retry count. One Event ingested with N
matching Endpoints produces N Deliveries.

### Attempt
A single HTTP request made in service of a Delivery. A Delivery may have multiple
Attempts (retries). Carries the response status/body of that one try.

## Avoid
- **"webhook"** as a noun — it conflates Endpoint, Delivery, and Attempt. Use the
  precise term instead.

Question 2 — The routing model: does every Endpoint get every Event, or do Endpoints subscribe to specific event types?

This is the root of the whole design tree — it determines your schema, your ingest flow, your fan-out logic, and what your test harness has to generate.

Your brain dump implies broadcast: an Endpoint is just {id, url}, an Event is just {id, data}, and ingest is "for each webhook url, create an event_log." No notion of event types or filtering. That's the simplest thing that satisfies the literal requirement ("invoke each registered webhook URL").

But real webhook systems are typed: an Event has a type like order.created, and an Endpoint subscribes to a set of types (["order.*"]). Fan-out only hits Endpoints that asked for that type. This is more realistic and demonstrates more taste, but it adds a matching concept, a subscription-list field, and more harness surface area.

My recommendation: go typed, but keep it minimal. Give each Event a type string, give each Endpoint an event_types list (with ["*"] meaning "all"). Exact-match only — no wildcards/globbing beyond the literal *. This buys you a realistic routing story and a more interesting dashboard/harness for very little code, and you can mention glob matching as "future work." It also makes the fan-out logic worth showing off.

The cost: one extra column, a tiny matching function, and the harness needs to pick types. The risk of broadcast: it's too trivial and the reviewer sees nothing interesting in your routing.

Do you want typed routing with exact-match + *, full broadcast, or something in between?
